Genai Acknowledgement

How AI Was Used

I employed AI assistance in four key areas of my assignment. First, I used AI to help formulate interview questions based on specific characteristics I provided. This approach allowed me to generate well-structured questions, able to extract the desired information. Second, I utilized AI for simulating interviews by providing detailed descriptions of individuals to represent. This produced comprehensive responses that, while occasionally more detailed than realistic interviews might yield, offered valuable insights for the project.

For the technical aspects of my assignment, I used AI to create CSS helper classes based on specific criteria required, which proved effective for my needs. Additionally, I used AI to convert my interview content into properly formatted HTML, rapidly reducing the time spent doing mindless tasks.

Finally, I engaged AI to brainstorm potential ethical risks associated with incorporating a newsletter on a webpage. Importantly, I critically reflected on each AI-generated suggestion and modified them to align with my personal perspective and understanding of the ethical considerations involved.

How AI Supported Learning

Using AI as a tool enhanced my learning experience rather than replacing my academic effort. When generating interview questions, I was able to focus on defining precise characteristics and requirements, which deepened my understanding of effective questioning techniques in research contexts. The process of providing detailed descriptions for simulated interviews required me to carefully consider the personas I wanted to represent, strengthening my understanding of user perspectives and interview dynamics.

Working with AI to create CSS helper classes improved my technical knowledge as I needed to define specific criteria. This process of providing requirements, reviewing AI output, and refining my approach enhanced my capacity to think about web design without getting held down by minor details.

Also useful to my learning was the ethical brainstorming process. By having AI generate potential ethical concerns and then critically evaluating each one, I was able to consider ethical risks which were not immdediately obvious to me. The process of adjusting AI suggestions to reflect my own viewpoint required deep engagement with the material and strengthened my critical thinking abilities.

Critical Evaluation of AI's Effectiveness

While AI proved to be a valuable tool throughout my assignment, its effectiveness varied across different applications. For interview question formulation, the AI performed exceptionally well when provided with specific characteristics, producing focused and relevant questions.

The simulated interviews using AI highlighted both strengths and limitations of the technology. The AI produced comprehensive responses that provided ample data for analysis. However, I observed that these responses were sometimes overly detailed and structured compared to authentic human interviews, which tend to include more natural variation, ambiguity and worse grammar. This limitation required me to adjust my analysis to account for this artificial consistency.

For CSS helper classes, AI proved highly effective when given specific criteria. The technical nature of this task aligned well with AI's strengths in code generation. Similarly, converting interview content to HTML was efficiently handled by AI, though I needed re-prompt due to my initial prompt not being specific enough.

The brainstorming of ethical risks demonstrated both the breadth and limitations of AI in conceptual thinking. While the AI provided a range of potential concerns, some lacked contextual relevance or failed to consider the specific context of my project. This required thorough engagement on my part to transform these ideas into meaningful ethical considerations.

Ethical Considerations

Using AI in academic work raises important ethical considerations that I was able to navigate throughout this assignment. First, I was mindful of the potential for AI to introduce bias in interview questions and simulated responses. To address this, I reviewed all AI-generated content critically and made adjustments to ensure fair representation and balanced perspectives.

Transparency is another key ethical consideration. Following the GenAI & MT Usage Framework guidelines, I have properly acknowledged all instances where AI was used in this assignment, ensuring academic integrity and honesty about the collaborative nature of the work.

I also considered the ethical implications of using AI-simulated interviews rather than conducting real interviews. While this approach offered practical benefits, such as huge time savings whilst still facilitaing learning, I recognized the importance of acknowledging the limitations of simulated data and that it is no substitute for real world data.

When using AI for ethical brainstorming, I was careful to avoid over-reliance on the technology's perspectives. By critically examining each suggestion and completely reformulating ideas to reflect my own understanding, I maintained intellectual ownership of the ethical analysis while benefiting from AI as a thought-provoking tool.

Throughout the entire process, I maintained a balance between leveraging AI capabilities and preserving my learning objectives. By using AI as a supporting tool rather than a replacement for critical thinking, I ensured that the final submission authentically represented my understanding, skills, and academic growth.
